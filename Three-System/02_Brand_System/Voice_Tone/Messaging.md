# THREE Voice & Tone Guide

## Voice (Consistent)

THREE's voice is:
- **Professional** - Credible and knowledgeable
- **Intelligent** - Smart but never condescending
- **Human-First** - Empathetic and accessible
- **Clear** - Direct and concise
- **Helpful** - Solutions-oriented
- **Confident** - Without being arrogant

## Tone (Varies by Context)

### Professional & Formal
**When**: System alerts, compliance notifications, official communications

*Example*:
> "Driver #42 has exceeded HOS limits. Immediate action required per FMCSA regulations."

### Conversational & Friendly
**When**: Onboarding, AI assistant, help text

*Example*:
> "Hey! I can help you optimize this route. Want me to find the best driver for this load?"

### Urgent & Direct
**When**: Critical alerts, safety issues, time-sensitive actions

*Example*:
> "⚠️ Vehicle 5 maintenance overdue. Take offline now."

### Supportive & Encouraging
**When**: Errors, help text, feature explanations

*Example*:
> "No loads yet. Create your first load to see real-time tracking in action!"

## Writing Style

### Do's ✓
- Use active voice: "Driver completed load" not "Load was completed by driver"
- Use contractions: "You've completed 12 loads" not "You have completed 12 loads"
- Use sentence case: "Create a new load" not "CREATE A NEW LOAD"
- Be specific: "Load delayed due to traffic on I-95" not "Load delayed"
- Use numbers: "12 drivers" not "twelve drivers"
- Address user directly: "Your fleet" not "The fleet"

### Don'ts ✗
- Don't use jargon without explanation
- Don't use exclamation marks (except for encouragement)
- Don't be vague: "Something went wrong" ← bad
- Don't blame user: "You forgot to..." ← make it "Please add..."
- Don't use ALL CAPS (except for acronyms: FMCSA, HOS, ELD)
- Don't use emojis except in chat context

## Localization

- Always maintain professional tone across languages
- Adapt metaphors for cultural context
- Preserve clarity above literal translation
- Test with native speakers
